Plants are one of five big groups (kingdoms) of
living things. They are autotrophic eukaryotes,
which means they have complex cells,
and make their own food. Usually they
cannot move (not counting growth).
Plants include familiar types
such as trees, herbs, bushes,
grasses, vines, ferns, mosses,
and green algae. The scientific
study of plants, known as botany,
has identified about 350,000 extant
(living) species of plants.
Fungi and non-green algae are not
classified as plants.
A plant needs sunlight, carbon dioxide,
minerals and water to make food by
photosynthesis. A green substance in
plants called chlorophyll traps the
energy from the Sun needed to make food.
